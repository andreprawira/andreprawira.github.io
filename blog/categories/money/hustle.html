Hustle

In this article, I will talk about hustling a.k.a how to get a little money as a side gig. Keep in mind that you, as an international student (F-1 and J-1) are legally not allowed to work outside school. You are allowed to do that with CPT (internship) or OPT (full time job after graduation). The lists of things you can do to earn money below are legal to borderline illegal, do it at your own discretion.

Money Rotation

When you come the first time to the States, you will need a bank to store your money. Based on my own experience, banks like Chase, Wells Fargo, local credit unions or Bank of America offers you a bonus if you store money with them. Usually, you need to store a minimum of $1500 and they will give you $150-$300 a couple days later. The catch is, you cannot close the account and you have to keep a minimum of $1500 for 90 days (3 months). Meaning, if on January you open bank with Chase and they give you $150, comes April you close the account and you open in Wells Fargo and you get another $150, repeat that in a year you can earn free $600, cuma perlu niat.

Selling Plasma and Group Study

Now, this is what you call mixing effort and desperation. In the States, there are clinics that will buy your plasma (like Bioplasma) for $50 and unlike donating your blood, you can do it every single day. The catch is, you will feel very tired and very weak. Also, I need to mention that it has been a debate whether international students (F-1 and J-1) are allowed to donate, some officials say yes and some dont. I personally dont because I dont need money that desperate but my friends have done it in the past and never got into any trouble (so far). You can also go to the STEM departments in your school, often the biology or physiology departments create a study (sometimes you have to smoke weed or do a certain exercise, or eat certain food) and they will give you money.

Stocks

If you have an extra money and would not mind risking your money, you are allowed to buy and sell stocks. I personally would recommend Robinhood because they offer $0 commission trades. Also, Robinhood is very user friendly and their blogs are concise and clear for new traders to use.

Financial Hardships

If for any reason, you and your family suffers an act of God incident that cripple you financially. You can file a financial hardship case to USCIS, if they approve your case, you are legally allowed to work outside school. However, you actually need cold hard facts that you are suffering financial hardship. Not some bullshit you can whip out in front of your parents.

Brave Browser

Now this is not much, but it is aleast something. If you have a personal device, whether it is your smartphone, tablet, or laptops. I guarantee you use browser (Safari, Chrome, Edge, etc). While using those browsers are free, they collect and sell your data. If you use Browser which uses Chromium (Chrome engine) you can opt-in to receive ads and get paid in form of a cryptocurrency (BAT). Again, it is not a lot and if you use browser a lot, in a year you probably can earn up to $15.
